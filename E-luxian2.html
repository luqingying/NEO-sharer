1.Wuhou Temple(Han Zhao Temple)

Introduction:National key cultural relics protection units, national AAAA-grade tourist attractions, national first-class museums.

Wuhou Temple is located in Wuhou District, Chengdu City, Sichuan Province. When it was first built in 223 when Hui Bei was built, it was the only monk and ancestral temple in China and the most prestigious heroes of the Zhuge Liang, Liu Bei and Hao Han heroes. It is also a national influence. The largest museum of the Three Kingdoms Relics. In 1961, the State Council announced the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units. In 2008, it was selected as the first batch of national first-class museums.

Related cultural story:Wuhouluo is a memorial hall for Zhuge Liang, the prime minister of the Sui and Han dynasties during the Three Kingdoms Period of China. He was named after Zhu Geliang’s death as Wu Xianghou.

In August, 3434, Zhu Geliang became ill due to overwork. He died of illness at Wuzhangyuan, the front line of the Northern Expedition, and was 54 years old. Zhuge Liang was an auspicious prime minister who was once called “Wuxiang Hou” before his death. After his death, he was later chastised by the monk and dynasty Liu Chan as “Zhongwu Hou”. Therefore, the temple was historically known as “Wuhou Temple”. The earliest Wuhou Temple in the country was in Ganxian County (Xian County) in Hanzhong, Shaanxi Province, but the most influential one is Wuhou Temple in Chengdu.

According to the records of the "Three Kingdoms", after Liu Bei died in Bai Di City in 223 CE, the halberd was transported back to Chengdu and was buried there. His name was Hui Ling. According to the Chinese system, there must be a temple in the tomb, so at the same time, there will be the birth of Han Zhaolie Temple.
