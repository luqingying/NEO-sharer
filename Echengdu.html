Du Fu Thatched Cottage Museum in Chengdu

Du Fu Thatched Cottage, the national key cultural relic protection unit, the national AAAA level tourist attraction, the national first level museum, the national key protection unit of ancient books. Du Fu Thatched Cottage, also known as the Huabu cottage, the Ministry of work, the Shao ling cottage, is located in the west suburb of Chengdu, the old residence of the great poet Du Fu of the Tang Dynasty in Chengdu, and the largest, most well preserved, most characteristic and famous place of the surviving Du Fu's trace remains. Du Fu has lived here for nearly four years, and more than 240 poems have been written so far. Among them, Wen Guan Jun's collection of Hebei in Henan has become a necessary course for students in many areas. The former residence of cottage is regarded as "holy land" in the history of Chinese literature.

At the end of the Tang Dynasty, the poet Wei zhuang found the thatched cottage site and rebuilt the hut to preserve it. All the dynasties in song, yuan, Ming and Qing Dynasties were renovated and expanded. The area of today's cottage covers an area of nearly 300 mu, still retaining the architectural pattern of Akihiro Ji's thirteen years (AD 1500) and the sixteen years of Qing Jiaqing (1811 AD) renovation. The architecture is simple and elegant and the garden is quiet and beautiful. It is a sacred place in the history of Chinese literature. In 1955, the memorial hall of Du Fu was established. In 1985, it was renamed the Du Fu Thatched Cottage Museum in Chengdu.

Wu hou Temple in Chengdu

Wu hou Temple, Chengdu, is the only imperial temple in China. It is composed of Wu hou Temple, Han Zhao Li temple and Hui Ling. It is customary to call the three as Wu hou Temple. Chengdu Wu hou Temple was built in 223 ad to build Hui Ling. Among them, Wu hou Temple was built before the Tang Dynasty and was adjacent to the temple of Han Zhao in the early days of Liu Bei. The Wu hou Temple was incorporated into the Ming Dynasty when the Ming Dynasty was rebuilt, forming a combination of the monarch and the minister, the temple and the mausoleum. In addition to Hui Ling, the main building of the ancestral temple was rebuilt in the Qing Dynasty in 1672. In 1961, the Wu hou Temple of Chengdu was declared the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units by the State Council. In 1984, the museum was established. In 2008, it was awarded the first batch of national first-class museums, enjoying the reputation of "Three Kingdoms". The Wu hou Temple Museum in Chengdu is now divided into three parts: Three Kingdoms historical sites, Western and Jin li Street folklore areas, with an area of 150 thousand square meters. In 2006, Wu hou Temple was awarded the national AAAA grade scenic spot. It is the most influential Three Kingdoms Museum in the world.

Kuan Alley and Zhai Alley

Kuan Alley and Zhai Alley, a historical and cultural area in Chengdu, Sichuan, China, consists of three eastern and Western streets (from north to south to south, narrow alley, and I Ko), as well as residents' homes between the streets. Kuan Alley and Zhai Alley was included in the conservation planning of historic and cultural cities in Chengdu in 1980s. In June 2008, the Kuan Alley and Zhai Alley renovation project, which lasted for three years, was completed. The renovation of the Kuan Alley and Zhai Alley is composed of 45 architectural communities, such as the courtyard house of the late Qing Dynasty and the beginning of the people's style, the garden building with the cultural and cultural background, the newly built house style The Inn Boutique and so on. In 2011, Kuan Alley and Zhai Alley was rated as one of the new ten sceneries in Chengdu.

The wide lane and narrow alley are the epitome of the ancient and young city of Chengdu, a symbol of deep memory. When tourists are accompanying the sunset, watching the smoke of the kitchen and walking in the alley of the dusk, a scene that is long gone from the old city to the citizenry life is floating in front of us.
