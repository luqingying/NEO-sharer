Jiuzhaigou

Jiuzhaigou National Nature Reserve is located in Nanping County, Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture of Sichuan province. It is more than 400 kilometers from Chengdu. It is a valley area of more than 40 kilometers deep. It is named for nine Tibetan villages, such as tree Zai Zhai, lotus leaf village and Chabu village, with a total surface of about 620 square kilometers, and about 52% faces. The product is covered by dense primeval forests. Jiuzhaigou is the first natural reserve to protect natural scenery as its main purpose. At the same time, it is the only place in China with the two crown of "World Natural Heritage" and "World Biosphere Reserve". Because of its unique original landscape, abundant animal and plant resources are known as "the world fairyland". Jiuzhaigou is the national key scenic spot and the national civilized scenic spot demonstration spot, and has been listed in the world heritage list.

Mount Emei

Mount Emei, the world cultural and natural heritage, the national key cultural relic protection unit, the national key scenic spot, the national AAAAA level scenic spot. Located in the city of Mount Emei, Leshan, Sichuan, the scenic area is 154 square kilometers, and the highest peak is at 3099 meters above sea level. With its steep terrain and beautiful scenery, it has the reputation of "showing the best in the world". The climate is diverse and the vegetation is rich. There are 3000 plants, including rare species in the world. Mountain road along the way there are more monkeys, often convoy to the tourists for food, for Emei a major feature. Mount Emei is one of the four famous Buddhist mountains in China. As the Dao field of the Pu Xian Bodhisattva, the main worship of the mongsts, there are about 26 temples, the important eight temples, and the frequent Buddhist events. On 1996 12 6, the Mount Emei Leshan Giant Buddha is listed as the world heritage list by UNESCO as the world's dual heritage of culture and nature.

Category: tourist attractions

Scenic area level: National 5A level scenic spot

Dujiang Dam

Dujiangyan is a world cultural heritage (included in the list of "world cultural heritage" by UNESCO in 2000), the world natural heritage (Sichuan giant panda habitat), the national key cultural relic protection unit, national scenic spot and national AAAAA tourist attraction. Dujiangyan, located in the west of Dujiangyan City, Chengdu, Sichuan Province, is located on the Minjiang River in the western part of the Chengdu plain. It was built in the last year of the Qin Zhao Wang (about 256 BC to 251 BC). It is a large water conservancy project built on the foundation of Li Bing and his son of Shu County, which is built on the basis of the predecessors of Bie Ling, which is composed of water fish mouth, flying sand weir, and the mouth of the bottle. For more than two thousand years, it has been playing the role of flood control and irrigation, making the Chengdu plain becoming a "land of abundance" from people and thousands of miles and droughts. Up to now, the irrigation area has reached more than 30 counties and cities, with an area of nearly ten million mu. It is the oldest and only retained in the world so far, and is still being used and characterized by no dam diversion. Cheng embodies the crystallization of diligence, courage and wisdom of the Han people in ancient China. The Dujiangyan scenic area mainly includes the view of volon, the second Wang Temple, the mount Lan Bridge, the jade base, the park, the jade base mountain park, the jade woman peak, the soul rock temple, the pzhao temple, the Cui Moon Lake and the Dujiangyan water conservancy project.
